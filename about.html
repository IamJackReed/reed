Thinking

How I Think About Change

I’m deeply interested in the people side of change. In my experience, most change efforts fail not because teams lack skill or motivation, but because problems are oversimplified, decisions drift away from reality, and learning gets replaced by compliance.

I take an empirical approach to complex work — creating feedback loops, making work visible, and treating uncertainty as something to explore rather than eliminate. Lasting improvement rarely comes from adopting Scrum “properly”. It comes from understanding the system of work, the incentives within it, and the behaviours it produces.

Practice

How I Work

At the team level

I coach teams toward self-management, where leadership is an attitude rather than a role. We build shared ownership, improve flow, and run small, high-value experiments — even when things are going well.

At the product level

I support Product Owners and product leaders to maximise value by improving visibility of work, outcomes, constraints, and technical debt — so trade-offs become explicit and decisions improve.

At the organisational level

I partner with leadership on strategy and operating-model change using agile-lean thinking and systems modelling. The goal is alignment between structures, incentives, and the behaviours needed to deliver.
